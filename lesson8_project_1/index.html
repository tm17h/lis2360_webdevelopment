Attending Florida State University

By: Thomas Martin

Americas Greatest College...

Florida State University is a public college located in the capital of Florida. Tallahassee, Florida is full of rich history and tradition dating back to 1821. Along with the history the wonderfull city possess, Tallahassee is home to one of the most well known colleges in the United States.

Florida State University is home to about 35,000 students from all across the country. FSU has many different degree fields, clubs, sporting teams, etc. There are plenty of ways for students to get connected and interact with others around campus. Our university does a tremendous job of hosting career events for upperclassmen to have a chance to apply for internships and even obtain jobs. Occasionally, their will be social events held on Landis Green that allows students to come play volleyball, cornhole, and enjoy some food and drinks with their fellow students.

This wonderfull city is also very well known for having a good time. Students have plenty of options when deciding what to do with their nights, whether its going to Greek life events or heading to the popular bars and clubs, there are always options. College Town is located right next to campus and is the most popular spot for our students. College Town has 4 popular restaurants that our students love: Madison Social, Township, Centrale Pizza, and Tin Lizzies. The weekends in the fall are never a disapointment because FSU is a huge football school and the students and our awesome fans all make their ways to Doak Campbell Stadium on Saturdays to watch our Seminoles play.

Activities in Tallahassee...

Tallahassee Museum The Tallahassee Museum of History and Natural Science is always one thing you must experience whenever you pass through. This museum displays several exhibits that features and demonstrates what life in Northern Florida was like throughout the 1800's
The State Park Alfred B. Maclay Gardens State Park is located off of Thomasville Road and is a Florida State Park as well as a Botanical Garden. This park was established in 1823 and is famous for its assortments of azaleas and dogwoods.
Capital Museum Tallahassee, Florida is the Capital of Florida and hosts the history Capital Museum. This museum showcases artifacts and photographs from the past history of Florida. This museum is located South Monroe Street in the Capital building.
St. Marks Lighthouse St. Marks Lighthouse was built in 1831 and is offically the second oldest Lighthouse in the state of Florida. This lighthouse has been rolocated and moved around many times due to weather conditions and erosion.
